History of Table Tennis

The Birth of Table Tennis

1880s

Table tennis originated in Victorian England as a parlour game played by the upper class.

Formation of the ITTF & First World Championships

1926

The International Table Tennis Federation (ITTF) was founded in Berlin, and the first World Table Tennis Championships took place in London, officially organizing the sport on a global level.

Ping Pong Diplomacy

1971

A surprise friendship between Chinese and American players during the 1971 World Championships in Nagoya, Japan helped thaw Cold War tensions and led to the historic visit of U.S. President Richard Nixon to China.

Table Tennis Joins the Olympics

1988

Table tennis made its Olympic debut at the 1988 Seoul Games, bringing greater global exposure and establishing it as a serious, elite-level sport.

Introduction of the 40mm Ball

2000

The ITTF changed the ball size from 38mm to 40mm to slow down gameplay slightly and make matches more exciting and easier to follow for audiences, especially on TV.

Some Stats of Table Tennis

300,000,000+

People play table tennis worldwide

With hundreds of millions of casual and competitive players, it’s one of the most globally accessible sports.

8000+

Maximum RPM on professional strokes

Elite players can generate over 8000 revolutions per minute (RPM) on forehand loops, making the ball curve unpredictably.

0.2

seconds to react to a smash

At the elite level, players have less than 0.2 seconds to respond to fast attacks, demanding extreme reflexes and precision.

40+

Olympic medals won by China since 1988

China has dominated Olympic table tennis, claiming nearly every gold and securing their legacy as the sport’s powerhouse.

226+

Countries affiliated with the ITTF

The International Table Tennis Federation has more member countries than the United Nations, showing how truly global the sport is.
